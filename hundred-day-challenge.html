Talking about me

Constandse.design is a one man design army, run by Chris Constandse. I am a full stack webdesigner, with a strong interest in everything digital. From photography to Treehouse's Javascript course. Besides my work I am a design mentor at trydesignlab.com where I guide students through the basics of visual design. I love to make people better through knowledge sharing. My end goal is to live and work in NYC. My next step? Learning as much as possible everyday to become the best in my field.

100 Day Challenge

One hundred days in a row was just the beginning. Having multiple variants and optimisation rounds made this project much more than a simple 100 day challenge.

Best work

Day 3

The idea of having an app that just focuses on selling tickets to only a certain city, really appealed to me. By focusing on simplicity and a good color contrast, I came up with the design above.

Day 18

Focusing on the simplicity of Google, I created the following design. Focussing on the material design colors and a lot of whitespace, I managed to pull of a design that would fit a promo-page of Google Cardboard just fine.

Day 26

By using strong imagery for a header, and a good contrast of the text, I created a very compelling composition. The user doesn't have to scroll to know what the page is about, and that was my goal.

Day 29

I used a similar image to day 26, though now I created a simple content block. By the colour usage, I created something that fits the whole layout very well with a couple of highlights in the form of orange.

Day 33

Stepping away from the minimalism, I created a contact form that focused more on strong elements that attract attention. I created this by using a strong colour and a bold font that is strenghtened by the all caps variant.

Day 36

A simple layout of an article showcase, that mainly focusses on the image that is used. The combination of a thin serif font with a thin sans-serif font, works wonders here, and triggers the attention of the viewer.

Day 39

Napster, the app that was extremely popular in the 2000s, got a new rebranding. By focussing on strong red colours, I created a new brand that will beat Spotify by awesomess and quality.

Day 56

A very minimalistic approach to the layout of an average article. By using colours that fit the image I created a consistent layout. As an addition, the image has a very strong balance that uplifts the minimalistic whole even more.

Day 57

The combination of a strong dark background element with a minimalistic approach in typography and visual hierarchy works wonders here. The very simple grid triggers enough attention at the user's eye to interact.

Day 60

A music player that focusses on album art instead of playlists and social streams. Album art is a form of art, and is often subservient to other elements in music players. In this design, I wanted to showcase a new an different way of communicating music.

Day 62

Two posters I created for Puma. Focusing on the brand colours of Puma, the design is recognizable from a far distance. Also by using strong copy, it focusses on the people who are on the edge of deciding wether they should pick up a new sport.

Day 66

The design of an app that focusses on the opening hours of public places. By using a minimalistic approach, I only focus on the bare minimums in information sharing. Less is more.

Day 67

A new way of completing your creditcard details by showcasing the product you're actually buying in combination with the required creditcard information. By using a simple but communicative layout (green ticks, big blue button) I make the UX as simple as possible.

Day 69

Minimalism is key here. It is a design for a exclusive airport lounge, and by the usage of thin lines and a thin font, I create a certain type of luxury. Also the appealing image in the background with a lot of gold and brown tones create a sense of exclusivity.

Day 75

This is a very experimental layout of creating a contact form. By using a lot of dark colours, and a lot of grey tints, I create a very interesting background. However, by putting the required information in white, it creates a layered effect with the right attention to the right elements.

Day 80

An interesting way of using colours for content blocks on a website. It creates just enough contrast to make the text properly readable, though it also makes the image much more of an interesting element to the user because it is much more of a visible element.

Day 84

A very simple but stylistic approach to airline tickets. A one colour based design makes this very interesting and gives the design a clean, grid structured layout that is very attractive to the user's eye.

Day 87

A poster layout for a new Nike store. By using very strong imagery in combination with interesting copy that fits the brand Nike perfectly, I create an interesting combination of elements that will definitely pull the user towards itself.

Day 91

ABN Amro is one of the biggest banks in the Netherlands, and re-creating their mobile app sounded just right. It focuses on a lot of information at the same time, and because of that it is mainly focussed on the heavy mobile internet users with multiple accounts.

Day 100

A simple but effective facebook reader app for the tablet market. no nonsense, just your timeline and your messages. By creating a strong contrast with the white background and the dark text, it makes it easy to read for the user and very scannable at the same time.
